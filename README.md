# learning-julia-physics
Easily accessible space for me to document and store my adventures in learning to use Julia for physics. I currently forsee this being a solo endeavor, but I'm not opposed to collaborating with my friends.

My initial plan is to go through [_Numerical Methods for Physics_](http://www.algarcia.org/nummeth/nummeth.html) by Alejandro L. Garcia.
This is motivated simply by the book being in front of me for a long stretch where I'm confident based on timings that it won't be used for a course.
I intend to work through all the examples I have the time for amidst my PhD work, and upload them here as a repository of Pluto Notebooks. 
Pluto Notebooks are very cool, their Python equivalent - Merimo notebooks - surprise me for not being more widely adopted.
Perhaps a parallel development with Merimo might be in order.
